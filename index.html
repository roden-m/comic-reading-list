Watchmen (1986) 12 Alan Moore Dave Gibbons DC 10 7.5
  • In a league of its own. The prose is a lot more sophisticated than any other comics I've read. A small detail I liked is the transitions like the ones into flashbacks. Only drawback is that I don't like such a heap of cynicism without hope. However, objectivebly speaking, this is a real masterpiece.
  • Incredible character work. Dr Manhattan is cold and distant, only caring for Laurie. His power is so otherworldly it alienates him from humans. Dr Manhattan is a god-man who regards human matters as insignificant. But should absolute power always alienate? The rich ruler is definintely separated from the needs of the common man. Politicans today are critiqued of being out of touch at times. But unlike Dr Manhattan, the God-man that is Jesus shows its possible to be almighty but also Emmanuel and El Roi.
  • Rorshach is another great character. You follow his slow descent into becoming a violent vigilante. But you still root for him because of his sad back story and the fact that he is active about injustice.
  • Laurie strikes me as a character that doesn't know herself well. She gets into the superhero stuff because of her mum and she resbounds into the relationship with Danny.
Kingdom Come (1996) 4 Mark Waid Alex Ross DC 9.5 6.8 Does a great job of slowly escalating to a crescendo. Modern comics are so trivial with action scenes, but the build this was done well as well as the pay off. The leadership of Superman raised a lot of questions on how to lead well. Also does a great job in showing the issues that arise in enforcing the law, how much force do you use? What do you do with a superhero gone rogue?
Ultimate Spiderman (2000) 133 Bill Jemas, Brian Michael Bendis Mark Bagley Marvel 8.8 7.3 One of the first comics i read. This and Runaways. This is a real classic. Carnage was a memorable arc. I love the moment in the end when JJ Jameson finally recognised Spiderman as the hero he is.
Hulk Gray (2003) 6 Jeph Loeb Tim Sale Marvel 7.5 6.3  
Runaways (2003) 18 Brian K Vaughan Adrian Alphona Marvel 8.4 7.3 Coming of age. What do you do when your parents, the people that you are supposed to be safe with, turn out to be villains? This was one of the first comics I read. I identified with the complicated relationship with parents. Though my parents are a lot better than supervillains of course.
Runaways (2005) 30 Brian K Vaughan Adrian Alphona Marvel 8.4 7.3  
Young Avengers (2005) 12 Allan Heinberg Jim Cheung Marvel 8.2 7.9 A story about teens trying to figure out how to have their own identities when they are the descendants of famous people. Will they always live in their shadow? Or will they forge their own path? Do they have to be a superhero like their parents? All these questions are raised and it makes for an interesting story. The kids won't be able to shake their association with their parents.
Civil War (2006) 7 Mark Millar Steve McNiven Marvel 8.8 8.0 A story about division, about superheroes being held accountable. It's not abundatly clearly who you should side with. More and more unity is becoming more of a personal value to me, so a story like this definitely resonates with me. A tragedy occuring and the legislative response and debate that ensues, mirrors a myriad of real life events. Raises important questions like how can you reconcile and both get what you want? How do you resolve a conflict that has become so pervasive? The sudden escalation to violence is often the fruit of division. The constant shifting of allegiances is a good device to show how tricky the moral dilema is. Spiderman is a key part in Tony's plan, then even he switches sides. Even Sue Richards switches sides.
Daredevil (2011) 36 Mark Waid, Fred Van Lente Marcos Martin Marvel 9.0 8.0 I've been sleeping on Daredevil, he is an awesome character. He interacts with the world in a unique way, through sonar. He has a profession outside of the superhero gig. In every battle, his every move is calculated. As a lawyer he defends people who need the help. There's a lot to like about the character. This has been a great entry point.
Blue Beetle (2011) 16 Tony Bedard, Keith Giffen Ig Guara Barros DC 8.0 8.0 Blue Beetle has cool powers. The relationship between him and his scarab is interesting, he has to submit to its control in a way. He doesn't have full control over it which makes for an interesting dynamic.
The Strange Talent of Luther Strode (2012) 6 Justin Jordan Tradd Moore Image 7.8 9.0 Pipsqueak gets strong. Very violent moments. Art is awesome.
East of West (2013) 45 Jonathan Hickman Nick Dragotta Image 8.8 8.8
Superman: Last Son of Krypton (2013) Grahpic Novel Geoff Johns, Richard Donner Adam Kubert, Gary Frank DC 9.0 9.2 Zod showed up out of nowhere and sent Supes to the Phantom Zone. Wrecked him. The idea of the Phantom Zone, a place where time stops is something real scary. Another child (Chris/Lord-Zod) appearing out of the woodworks felt a bit on the nose, but with that being said, it was an interesting dynamic, Superman immediately drawn to protecting him as he was once that kid. A really great, beginner friendly read for Superman. Brainaic has very clear motives. His intention to capture socities, shrink them, and leech them of their knowledge is very fitting of a super villain.
Birthright (2014) 50 Joshua Williamson Andrei Bressan Image 9.3 9.0 When it was revealed that the ‘Nevermind’ had infected Mikey that really surprised me and motivated me to read on. One of the themes is that destiny has flexibility. It makes you question who is the good guy here? The supposed good people make decisions that sacrifice lives. But Mikey has the ‘Nevermind’ corrupting him, so he couldn’t possibly be good. Changing sides, and the tension of who is on what side is a theme. The prospect of Terrenos leaking into earth always looms over you. The art on the full-page spreads is awesome.

Superman Smashes the Klan (2019) 5 Gene Luen Yang Gurihiru DC 9 8.3 Awesome story about not being ashamed of who you are. The little girl and Superman’s story parallels. The little girl ashamed of her Asian heritage, Superman ashamed of being an alien. But they both come to accept who they really are. They both embrace what makes them different which unlocks new power for them. For Superman his superpowers are fully released, for the little girl, she gains new courage.
House of X/Power of X (2019) 12 Jonathan Hickman Pepe Larraz, RB Silva Marvel 9.6 8.8 My first Hickman comic! Wahoo! Crosses over into Sci-Fi territory with the different timelines and advanced technology. Complicated story, even the island itself has lore. The many lives of Moira are very interesting. Introduces Krakoa, a mutant promised land. Interesting that Charles would give up the idea of co-habitation. Resurrecting mutants by combining the powers of several mutants was crazy. Art really suits the story. Landscape of Krakoa is awesome.
