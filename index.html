He has Faustus try to distort his memories.Lukin is cursed with Red Skull in his mind. Agent 13 is brainwashed by Dr Faustus. Peggy has dementia.
  • Another theme is switching sides. Bucky switches sides. Faustus switches sides in the end. Agent 13 is brainwashed onto the other side. The 50's Captain America is on the evil side.

But Cap knows there is some vestiges of the real Bucky, which makes sense since he rebels against his programming. What does justice look like for Bucky considered he was programmed by the Soviets? That gets into the territory of the philosophy of mind. How much freedom does one have? To what degree is Bucky morally culpable for his actions?

He's such an interesting character because he has a horrible past, but he starts a new chapter after he regains his mind. He is almost reborn into a new life. He wants to be like Cap. He was the vessel that took so many lives but he still feels the need to make up for what he did. That tension drives him, but its not something that could ever be completely fulfilled.

  • There isn't any a great tie of the story as whole. But i guess it is more so a series of short narratives. Nevertheless, overall, one of my favourite comics, maybe a ten, i'll think about it.

A Note on Ratings

In rating all these different comics a few issues have arisen.

One, I need a guiding criteria. My ratings can fluctuate based on mood. Preventing this can be done with a guiding criteria. Hopefully this bears fruit of a more consistent rating system. But I prefer the language of what I value rather than criteria. Criteria seems mechanical.So I ask myself, what do I value in art, what do I value in narrative?

  • In art, I value vibrancy. Because why not? Along with vibrancy, there is a feeling you get that this comic is full of life. Obviously some art will be dreary as a stylistic choice. The story takes on melancholic themes, or, perhaps the protagonist demands it. I remember a story in Superman Red and Blue where all was in grey, then in a climactic moment red and blue erupted on the page. Really well done. That being said, for my taste, I value vibrancy.
  • Some art seems experimental and especially creative. You can play with proportions and create different caricatures of characters. That is easy.
  • In story, I appreciate when there is a real set up for the battle. I want to know the stakes. Abitrary action is a plague on modern reading. A highlight for a clean set up of the stakes of a battle is Kingdom Come. There was an escalating tension followed by an epic battle. The way it should be done.
  • In art, detail can be a more objective way to give a rating.

Two, an issue of progressive reading and relativity. As I read more, my scale will be reset. Everything is relative. And if everything is relative, and you add in new reference points, all else is effected. So, I do expect some inconsistency to set in. It's inevitable I suppose. Nevertheless, I will try to update ratings in light of new reads. A great example of this is Watchmen. Relative to others, this blows everything out of the water. This ten is a very different ten, to another ten or high nine that I have given in the past.
